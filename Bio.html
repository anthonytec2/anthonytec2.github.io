Bio-Electrocardiogram Amplifier

Above is the final project that was developed in my biomedical circuits and signals class. The project consists of an Electrocardiogram amplifier with custom MATLAB software in order to detect the BPM of a patient. The Electrocardiogram amplifier consists of 3 stages: Instrumentation Amplification stage, Low Pass stage and a High Pass stage. The reason that an instrumentation amplifier is used in the first amplification rather than an op amp is due to the low noise and high impedance characteristics. These characteristics are ideal for biosignal amplification due to their very low amplitude in the millivolt range. After the amplification occurred an active low pass filter was created by creating an input resistance to the op amp and then a resistor and capacitor in parallel feeding that input to the output. This stage was then fed into a high pass filter by placing a capacitor to ground and resistor in series with the op amp input terminal and an additional resistor connecting that terminal to the output. Through the effect of all the above a band pass filter was created which amplified the necessary biological content. Seen in the above photos in the output of this circuit elements. After this system was done, the output was digitized using a National Instruments analog to digital converter. This data was then acquired and the fast Fourier transform was taken of a short portion of this signal. Through this then the peak was identified in the 0-5Hz range which represents the beats per second of a patient. Then through dimensional conversions this BPS was converted into a BPM and displayed via matlab.
